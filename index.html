THE BILL HALL MEMORIAL FUND








Bill attended and graduated from Galena Schools with the class of 1955 and received his Bachelor of Science in Education in 1961 and his Master’s in Education in 1972 from Pittsburg State University. He began and ended his 30 year career with the Joplin Schools teaching at North Junior High School , Memorial High School and Joplin Jr. High. Bill also served in the Army Reserves beginning in 1958.



Bill became a member of the Galena Volunteer Fire Department in 1956 and continued his membership for the next 64 years holding positions of Secretary, Assistant Chief and since 1985 serving as Galena’s Fire Chief. Bill’s dedication to the department was an extension of his love of his hometown, Galena. Bill’s desire was always to make things “bigger and better” and demonstrated that by his tireless work constantly upgrading equipment and training the men and women of the department whom he loved like his own family. Bill’s passion for Galena Day’s, an annual birthday celebration for the town of Galena, helped not only to promote Galena but to generate funds for the addition and improvement of equipment for the fire department. Bill served as the master of ceremonies for the celebration for 40 years and worked to provide a fun and entertainment for all to enjoy. Bill was an active participant of the Fire Chief’s Association and was proud of the camaraderie that the area fire departments shared. Bill lived by his motto “Make a Difference”. This Motto can now be seen on all of Galena Fire Departments vehicles.







Bills commitment to his community included his involvement in the Galena Lion’s Club, a lifetime member of the Galena Mining and Historical Museum, even donating the original “Katy Station” building where the museum is housed today. Bill was a faithful member of the First Baptist Church in Galena for 45 years where he held various positions but his most treasured was as the door greeter where he welcomed everyone with a smile, a hand shake and his sense of humor.
